A recent survey has revealed a concerning trend among secondary school pupils in England: their critical thinking skills are declining due to the increasing reliance on artificial intelligence (AI). Two-thirds of teachers reported that students are losing essential abilities such as writing, problem-solving, and even basic conversation skills, largely attributed to the convenience of AI tools like voice-to-text technology.

Teachers are expressing skepticism about the government's push for AI tutoring tools, fearing that these technologies may undermine the value of human interaction in education. While AI can be a powerful resource for lesson planning and administrative tasks, the lack of proper training and policies for its use in schools raises significant concerns about its impact on student learning and engagement. As educators grapple with these challenges, the question remains: how can we balance the benefits of AI with the need to foster critical thinking and creativity in our students?

The future of education may hinge on our ability to integrate technology responsibly, ensuring that it enhances rather than replaces the foundational skills that prepare students for success in a complex world. Are we ready to navigate this digital revolution in a way that truly benefits our learners?
