Enterprises across the Asia-Pacific (APAC) region are set to significantly boost their investments in artificial intelligence (AI), with a staggering 96% of organizations planning to increase their spending by an average of 15% by 2026. This shift is not just about enhancing productivity; it reflects a broader change in business priorities, with revenue growth now taking precedence over traditional operational goals.

The findings from Lenovos CIO Playbook 2026 study reveal that the hype surrounding AI is transitioning into tangible projects, with 66% of organizations either piloting or systematically adopting AI technologies. Notably, the report highlights a shift in funding sources, as non-IT departments increasingly finance AI initiatives, indicating a collaborative approach between IT and business teams.

As organizations pivot towards AI inferencingwhere models are used to make real-time predictionsCIOs must be mindful of the associated costs, which can be significantly higher than training expenses. With 86% of APAC organizations opting for a hybrid AI approach to meet data sovereignty requirements, the landscape of AI adoption is evolving rapidly. As we look to the future, the rise of agentic AI systems capable of independent decision-making could redefine how businesses operate. Are we ready to embrace this next frontier of AI?
