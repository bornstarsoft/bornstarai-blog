The recent volatility in the stock markets, particularly in the US and Asia, highlights growing concerns about an AI bubble. Despite strong performances from tech giants like Nvidia and robust economic indicators, investor jitters have led to a significant decline in major stock indexes. The S&P 500, for instance, has dropped over 4% this month, signaling a potential market correction.

Analysts are particularly worried about the concentration of market power among a few tech companies, often referred to as the 'magnificent seven.' This concentration can lead to increased volatility, especially when investor sentiment shifts. As fears about inflated AI valuations persist, the question remains: will the market stabilize, or are we on the brink of a deeper correction?
